Research Interests

My interests lie in the interface between computer science and economics. I study how the designer of multi-agent systems (such as data-annotation platforms, academic conferences, and recommender systems) can do better by taking care of the strategic behaviors of agents. In particular, I design better mechanisms to elicit and evaluate high-quality information and how the information can benefit the system. More specifically, my research interests include information elicitation and aggregation, crowdsourcing, recommender systems, peer grading, peer reviewing, and their interactions with generative AI.
